## Levers used and findings

- **The original drafts were wildly too easy.** The plan-sketch configs (20-30 moves, counts 10-20 on 4 colors) simulated at 100% for both policies; greedy clears a 4-color 6x6 goal of 12 in ~2.5 moves because cascades collect 4-5 goal tiles per move.
- **colorCount was the decisive lever, not moves.** On 4 colors the greedy/random efficiency gap is ~2.5x, so any budget tight enough to pull greedy under 95% drives random to <5% — the two bands are mutually exclusive on colorCount 4, which is why no shipped level uses it. At 5 colors the gap narrows enough for a wide overlap on 6x6, and 6 colors (adds orange to the palette) narrows it further, making the hard tail (007-010) possible.
- **Board size cuts the other way:** 7x7 favours greedy over random (more board to exploit), so 7x7 levels (004, 008) needed a moves cushion or 6 colors to keep random >= 0.15.
- **Multi-color goals are much easier per total tile than single goals** (any cascade progresses some goal), so 2- and 3-goal levels carry higher totals. They also starve the random policy fastest — 005 and 010 are the two levels where random sits closest to the 0.15 floor, and the 010 finale needed asymmetric counts to stay in band (12+12+12 measured random at 13.2%). Pass-1 (n=500) shipped 12+12+11; the 2026-07-10 pass-2 re-measure below showed that config was really 13.9% and trimmed it to 12+11+11.
- **giftMoves 5 dominates random's wins:** random finishes via gift in >90% of its wins on most levels; greedy leans on gift increasingly through the tail (32% -> 77%).
- **Surprise:** 6-color 6x6 boards deadlock-shuffle in ~12-17% of runs (vs <2% elsewhere) — the shuffle mechanic is load-bearing on 007/009/010.
