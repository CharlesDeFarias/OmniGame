## Levers learned

Decision #22 confirmed: obstacles make 15-25-move budgets calibratable at 5 colors — collect counts scale up (30-44) because boxes/ice gate collection and soak moves, so both policies stay in-band where plan-2's collect-only levels saturated. hp2 boxes (B) are worth roughly two hp1 boxes in difficulty but concentrate risk: they wall off regions longer, driving shuffle rates up on 6x6 boards — keep B-walls on 7x7 or keep 6x6 movable area ≥ 2/3. Ice is the gentlest obstacle (near-zero shuffle impact) and the best random-floor preserver: ice-heavy 011/016 show the highest greedy/random spreads. Gift usage runs 17-46% (vs up to 77% in 001-010): longer budgets mean fewer endings decided in gift territory, partially addressing the decision-#22 gift-feel caveat. Note: these numbers were produced across two agent sessions (authoring+iteration, then final 500-run record) — configs untouched between them.
